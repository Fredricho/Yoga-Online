ashtanga

Ashtanga Yoga is a traditional style of yoga that was developed by Sri K. Pattabhi Jois in the 20th century. The practice is characterized by a specific sequence of postures, or asanas, that are performed in a particular order, with a focus on breath and movement.

The Ashtanga Yoga sequence consists of six series of asanas, each with its own set of postures and variations. The practice is typically done to promote sweating and detoxification, and practitioners are encouraged to move at their own pace, while synchronizing their breath with each movement.

Ashtanga Yoga is often described as a physically demanding and rigorous practice, as it requires a great deal of strength, flexibility, and stamina. The primary series, also known as Yoga Chikitsa, or yoga therapy, focuses on building a strong foundation of postures and alignment, while the later series introduce more challenging postures and transitions.

Hatha

Hatha Yoga is a traditional style of yoga that is characterized by a focus on physical postures, breathing techniques, and meditation. The word "hatha" is derived from the Sanskrit words "ha" meaning sun and "tha" meaning moon, symbolizing the balance and union of opposing.

In Hatha Yoga, the emphasis is on balancing the body and mind through physical practice. The practice often begins with warm-up movements and breathing exercises, followed by a sequence of postures, or asanas, that are held for a period of time. These postures can range from gentle stretches to more challenging inversions and balances.

Overall, Hatha Yoga is a versatile and accessible practice that can offer a range of physical and mental benefits. Whether you're looking to increase flexibility, reduce stress, or deepen your connection with your body and breath, Hatha Yoga can be a great way to enhance your overall health and well-being.

yin

Yin Yoga is a slow-paced, meditative style of yoga that involves holding passive stretches for several minutes at a time. Unlike more active styles of yoga that focus on building strength and flexibility through movement and flow, Yin Yoga is designed to target the deeper connective tissues in the body, such as the ligaments, joints, and fascia.

In Yin Yoga, poses are held for anywhere from 1 to 10 minutes, depending on the practitioner's comfort level and experience. The long holds allow for a deeper release and stretch, which can help to increase flexibility, improve joint mobility, and reduce stiffness and pain in the body.

Overall, Yin Yoga can be a great complement to more active styles of yoga or other forms of exercise, as it provides a balance of deep stretching and relaxation. It can be a wonderful way to unwind, connect with the body, and cultivate a greater sense of inner calm and well-being.
